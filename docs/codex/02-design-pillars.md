# 02 — Design Pillars

## Liminal Frontier

Chornolis Marches is not just a forest adventure. It is a borderland: between settlement and wilderness, day and night, human and spirit, safety and danger, known and unknown.

The word **Marches / Порубіжжя** should guide the tone of the game. The player is always near a threshold, crossing from the ordinary world into something older, stranger, and less certain.

Ukrainian phrasing:

> Порубіжжя Чорнолісу — це не просто край лісу. Це межа між селом і дикістю, днем і ніччю, людським і нелюдським, безпекою і небезпекою, знаним і незнаним.

## Living Ecosystem

The world should not exist only as player content. Animals, NPCs, monsters, resources, weather, and settlements should eventually have their own rhythms.

Examples:

- wolves/foxes hunt prey;
- herbivores can overpopulate if predators are removed;
- resources can be gathered, depleted, or restored;
- player intervention can create ecological imbalance;
- NPCs and creatures should be more than vendors/enemies/scenery.

## Skills Grow Through Use

Characters should improve by doing things, not by choosing abstract levels.

- A hunter becomes better by hunting.
- A gatherer becomes better by gathering.
- A scout becomes better by moving, observing, and reading tracks.
- Progression should feel grounded in lived experience.

## Skills Grow Through Observation

Skills can also be learned by watching beings that are better at them.

Examples:

- watching a herbalist gather herbs can unlock or improve Herbalism;
- watching a hunter set a snare can teach Trapping;
- watching a wolf follow tracks can teach Tracking;
- watching a fox vanish into brush can improve Stealth;
- watching a forest spirit cross a boundary can hint at rare/mythic skills.

Observation should not always grant progress. It can depend on:

- observer attribute/attention;
- observer current skill;
- observed being's skill;
- clarity, distance, visibility;
- danger and pressure;
- whether the action is trained, natural, magical, or species-specific.

## No universal character levels

Avoid a classic “XP → level → perk” loop as the main progression model. Skills, attributes, knowledge, relationships, reputation, equipment, access, and world-state consequences should carry growth.

## Diegetic-first UI

Player-facing text should feel like the world speaking. Avoid exposing too many raw numbers unless the player uses debug/admin/technical mode.

## Incomplete Information

The player should not always know exact danger, HP, odds, ecology, and social state. Normal UI can use descriptive ranges, while debug tools may expose exact values.

## Social and faction direction

Longer-term ideas include:

- contacts, groups, курені, січі;
- reputation by people/factions rather than a single charisma/influence stat;
- social signals and observable behavior;
- trade with real prices/economy rather than an abstract Influence attribute.
