# Backlog

Accepted ideas that fit Chornolis Marches but are not the current focus.

Backlog differs from Icebox: backlog means “we probably want this, but not right now”; Icebox means “interesting, but too early, too large, or still uncertain”.

## Current backlog themes

- Ecology balancing after first rabbit reproduction: growth rates, overgrazing severity, migration/spread and visible degradation.
- Dedicated ecology pressure view: keep `/stat` as general `Статистика`, then add a separate page/command for deeper ecology, pressure and resource dynamics. It should focus on overgrazing, depleted locations, predator/prey pressure, regeneration, crowding, recent attack danger and regional health without crowding the general stats view.
- Pressure-aware resource regeneration: ordinary resources currently regenerate on a timer, but later regeneration should account for active pressure. If a location is being gathered, grazed or eaten from, recovery should pause or slow; if pressure stays higher than regeneration, resources should visibly worsen and can fall to zero. Quiet locations without recent gathering/grazing should recover more reliably.
- Predator reproduction depth: current fox/wolf reproduction is a simple regional check with adult breeding pair, prey-unit threshold and rare birth roll. Later, add visible mate-seeking, territory/den choice, pregnancy or nesting delay, parent/offspring proximity, season or calendar windows, and food-dependent litter viability. Foxes can look for mates and suitable burrows; wolves can form or maintain packs around deep-forest dens. Keep the first richer version observable through tracks, local signs and `/stat`, not only hidden counters.
- Vegetation spread and succession: grass already regenerates locally, but later healthy, high-grass wild locations should help neighboring compatible locations recover or grow. Quiet territory without heavy grazing should gradually overgrow, while sparse/depleted land should recover faster if adjacent cells have abundant grass and suitable biome/season/weather. Rich grass cover can later improve chances or max amounts for related resources such as лікарські трави, berries, moss and other low vegetation, while overgrazing should weaken those linked resources.
- Deeper natural vegetation recovery estimates: the first `Винищена трава` inspection now exists through the feature button and `/examine grass` / Ukrainian aliases. Later versions should derive a richer approximate range from recent grazing/gathering pressure, weather, season, moon/world time, grass amount/max, depleted-feature data and regional health. Keep exact tick math behind technical details; ordinary text should keep saying things like "за кілька годин", "не скоро", or "без дощу/спокою трава тут майже не відновиться".
- Active lisovyk restoration path: when the lisovyk wakes because a resource is depleted, he should travel toward exhausted locations in his region instead of wandering randomly. Movement and restoration should use queued actions with visible comments, tracks or ominous local signs. Locations where he stops or passes can gradually regain regeneration, clear `depleted_vegetation` exhaustion or receive a temporary restoration boost rather than recovering instantly. Players in his path may be threatened or attacked, especially if they are gathering, carrying fresh loot, or standing in a visibly damaged place.
- Low-prey warning through Дід Лісовик: when a region or the whole world loses all rabbits, mice or another core prey species, write a broad atmospheric warning so players understand the food chain is in trouble. If Дід Лісовик is awake, he can grumble directly about what predators will eat now; if he is asleep, frame it as a dream-mutter heard across the Порубіжжя. This should be rate-limited and should not wake or move him by itself.
- Animal-restoration offering loop: promote a near-term, player-accessible way to recover small prey populations without admin tools. Add small authored features such as hare statues, mouse stones, carved burrow markers or other forest charms where players can leave berries, herbs or another fitting offering. After a queued delay and cooldown, if local or regional rabbit/mouse population is below a safe threshold, create a pair of young animals or a small bounded group nearby. Keep it diegetic and capped: offerings should not become a predictable shop or unlimited spawn button, and failed/too-early attempts should leave a sign or flavor response rather than silently doing nothing.
- Biome-specific herbivore fauna after the rabbit/mouse test loop: forest, dry luka and riverbank should not share the same animal set.
- Animal hunger/starvation tuning: the first hunger deaths and `/stat` counters exist, but thresholds, species-specific tolerance, migration before death, hunger-driven herbivore grazing, predator desperation, and region-level starvation pressure still need observation and balancing.
- Visible animal migration follow-up: show animal movement in clients through aggregated local signs, tracks, `/stat` deltas or opt-in/debug views instead of one Telegram message per animal step.
- Budgeted and aggregated creature simulation: cap individual creature thinking per world tick, process background animals by location/species/diet where possible, and reserve individual `WorldAction` rows for visible, important or dangerous actors.
- Weather/magic ecology hooks: rain and restoration magic should shorten exhausted-vegetation recovery.
- Dynamic magical-rest cap: the current admin/magic-fire rest multiplier is a config constant, but later it can vary by moon phase, sacred day, holiday or world event.
- Day/night and light.
- Campfires and firewood.
- Darkness creature encounter: add a magical creature that appears in darkness, leaves a шаг when calmly watched or tracked, and disappears/reappears elsewhere in the region when light reaches the місцина or when active social/noisy actions such as greetings and signals scare it. Simple looking should not scare it. If scared, it should not leave useful tracks.
- Early `/respawn` support.
- Starter settlement and NPCs.
- Guard/warden NPC: add a camp/bridge guard who patrols the bridge and starter settlement, visibly watches the approaches, and attacks predators that enter protected areas. This should be an early example of a useful NPC with territory and purpose, not only dialogue.
- Fishing: riverbank fishing loop, fisher NPC, and learning basic fishing by watching or speaking with them.
- Basic crafting.
- Barter and trade.
- Use- and observation-based skill progression.
- Starter learner cloak: consider giving new characters a simple cloak, likely an `учнівський плащ` or similar subtype, as early clothing and a diegetic marker of the first learning path. This should connect to the first learning/observation skill, so the character can begin learning how to learn and later use observation, apprenticeship or practice to master other skills. Keep it atmospheric rather than a generic starter item.
- Follow/observe target action: when examining an animal, character or other creature, offer `Слідувати` as an action. The first version can simply keep the player moving after the target and observing their actions; later it should connect to group travel, apprenticeship, stealth/safety checks and learning skills through observation.
- Animal experience and titles: successful hunters can become more dangerous or recognizable, such as `материй вовк` or `досвідчена лисиця`.
- Tracks, stealth and visibility.
- Track output UX and Ukrainian grammar: `/track` should stay a quick scan with natural direction lines and no explicit age, while `Роздивитися` under track results, `/examine tracks`, `роздивитися сліди` and later per-track aliases such as `роздивитися вовчий слід` should show richer details from stored `WorldTrack.createdAt`: freshness, confidence, direction, whether several tracks likely belong to the same creature, and skill-gated details. Continue improving cases for named characters and group repeated similar tracks when many point the same way. Once world time advances properly, track age and similar relative-time text should describe in-game time rather than real server/player wall-clock time.
- Creature and item grammar registry: centralize sex-aware and case-aware display forms for animals, NPCs, players, corpses, resource types and inventory objects. UI code should ask for context-specific Ukrainian forms instead of hardcoding keys such as `corpse_rabbit_female`; reuse `creatureForms` and player forms where possible, store generated item metadata for legacy inventory/resource types, and cover nominative/genitive/accusative plus plural/count forms.
- Creature aggression and defense: wolves can threaten people, smaller predators can defend themselves, and herbivores may fight if cornered or protecting offspring.
- Admin/debug permissions and moderation hardening after the first scribe/admin gate: `/reset`, `/tickSet`, `/cleanupCreatures`, `/addCampfire`, `/addTorch`, `/addTwigs` and protected web views now have a first access-control layer, but still need better audit logging, clearer role management, review of any leftover dangerous paths, and a smoother admin UX before broader testing. Include a scribe name-approval workflow: a list such as `/all character` for characters needing review, service-profile buttons `Схвалити ім’я` / `Не схвалити ім’я`, and a rejection path where the scribe can write a short explanation to the character.
- Character/account protection: add an account model beyond Telegram-only identity, including email/password login, character ownership, and a controlled way for an authorized user/admin to enter as a character when needed.
- Richer auto-mode profiles and player-configurable auto behavior: keep the current `Авто` behavior as a gathering/foraging-oriented profile, then add a separate auto-hunting profile that walks, looks for prey and attacks like a hunter/archer. Later versions should extend hunting with tracking, traps, ranged weapons and safety rules.
- NPC admin behavior controls: extend the new NPC service card beyond read-only details into actual behavior settings. Future controls should cover more than a simple auto toggle: profession route, hunting/gathering/rest behavior, aggression/guard rules, teaching availability, visibility/debug flags, and temporary event overrides.
- Ukrainian interface terminology pass: `Озирнутися`, `Місцина`, `Роздивитися`, and similar diegetic wording instead of direct English/RPG calques.
- Random pre-generated Ukrainian names with database uniqueness checks.
- Name approval workflow for custom names and their case forms. Scribes should be able to review a general character list, open a character profile, approve the displayed name/case forms, or reject them with a message explaining what to fix.
- Onboarding hardening: Ukrainian-only custom names, correct apostrophes/dashes, stripping emoji, bidi/right-to-left controls and invisible characters, and escaping user-entered names before display.
- Foraging/firewood loop: broaden `/gather` without arguments into a general local foraging/search action while keeping exact resource variants such as `/gather herbs`, `/gather berries` and `/gather mushrooms`. Possible finds include хмиз / dry sticks, dry branches, moss, medicinal moss variants, animal bones, small coin finds such as шаги, and rare ґривня or a few ґривні. Results should depend on region, biome, visibility and special location conditions/features, so a riverbank, dry luka, forest edge, bridge, den entrance or old camp can produce different tables and chances.
- Pickable хмиз expansion: the first visible `twigs` / хмиз seed pass exists in selected forest and dry-luka locations. Later, broaden it across most forest locations and a smaller number of luka/old-camp locations, preferably through biome-aware foraging rather than only authored seed objects.
- Campfire fuel depth: the first `Додати хмиз` loop consumes carried хмиз and can extend or prepare ordinary campfires, but later a згасле вогнище should track richer fuel state: dry grass, twigs, larger branches, wet fuel and smoke. Better fuel should extend burn time differently, affect smoke/light, and eventually matter for crafted campfires.
- Ground money and small find objects: support authored and reset-seeded loose coins such as `ґривня` and `шаги` as objects in a location. They should be visible through ordinary `/look` when light/visibility allows, discoverable through `/examine` when dark, inspectable like corpses/other objects, and pickable into `Речі`.
- Interactive location features: make authored map features more than passive description text. A feature such as `Кам’яне кільце майже заросло травою. Знизу тягне холодом, але води не чути.` should be inspectable through `/examine` and can expose contextual actions based on state, carried items, money, time, visibility or prior choices. Example: if the player has a `шаг`, show `Кинути шаг`; the coin is removed, a trace/event is recorded on the feature, and later, when the character actually sleeps in a future sleep mode, something may come in a dream and repay the offering. Keep this diegetic and consequence-driven rather than a generic feature menu.
- Shrines and offerings: add authored sacred-place features such as `капище`, roadside stones, old tree-altars, carved posts or quiet ritual clearings. They should be inspectable location features and can expose offering actions based on carried items: `Покласти хмиз`, `Кинути шаг`, `Лишити трави`, `Покласти здобич` and similar. Offerings should remove or transform the item, record a local feature trace/world event, and later affect dreams, omens, spirits, local safety, weather/calendar hooks or reputation with unseen powers. Keep this separate from generic shops/quests: the player should not see a predictable price list, only signs, memory and consequences. Button/action parity matters: add `/offer`, `/offer <item>`, `пожертвувати`, `лишити дар`, `покласти дар`, plus feature-targeted aliases when this becomes playable.
- Decide whether foraging stays separate or becomes a broader `/gather` mode with target categories.
- Visible local pickup/gather feedback: when a character picks up a corpse, coin, dropped item or other ground object, or gathers visible resources such as herbs, berries, mushrooms, firewood, moss or loot, nearby characters in the same location should receive an observer message. Use Ukrainian gender/number-aware phrasing such as `Такий-то підібрав/підібрала/підібрали труп зайця`, `підняв/підняла шаг`, `зібрав/зібрала лікарські трави`; keep exact quantities hidden unless debug/detail mode allows them. This should reuse the same social-message pattern as speech/greetings, with separate actor and observer wording.
- Speech range and privacy modes beyond `/say`: add distinct commands/actions for `шепотіти`, `кричати` and a louder world/announcement-like mode such as `галасувати` or another better Ukrainian verb chosen during UX pass. `/say` should remain local to the current location. Whisper should target one character: the target receives the full quote, the actor gets confirmation, and other characters in the location only see a vague observer line such as `Хтось щось шепоче, але ви не чуєте деталей.` Shout/cry should reach the region with appropriate distance/visibility limits later; the loudest mode may reach the whole world or a global admin/event channel, but should have stamina/cooldown/social consequences so it does not become spam. All speech modes should write structured `/chat` events with speaker name, channel/range, target when present, location/region/world scope and quote text.
- Directed reply flow for addressed speech: add `/reply` as a way to answer the latest text addressed directly to the player, preserving the original speaker/target context when possible. Telegram messages such as `Аїд сказали вам:\nДоброго здоров'я, подорожній.` should eventually include inline actions: `Відповісти` for one of several generated/contextual quick replies, and `Відповісти як...` for prompting the player to write custom text that is then sent as the reply. Store enough metadata on speech/chat events to know which message is replyable, who is being answered, whether the player is still in range, and how the response should appear to the actor, target and nearby observers.
- Expand local technical details beyond the first scribe-only `/debugGet` and `/debugSet <0|1>` pass. The toggle is per character, not global: exact numbers, hidden counters, attributes, skills, queue/resource values and similar diagnostics should appear only to scribes/admins who enabled them. Ordinary UI should keep using descriptive states such as healthy, wounded, exhausted, `скоро зіпсується` or `ідеально`.
- Shared command/action registry for Telegram buttons, Ukrainian aliases and future clients: the 0.11.5 alias layer gives most player-facing buttons command equivalents, but later this should become a central registry that defines canonical action ids, slash commands, Ukrainian/MUD-style phrases, button labels and handler wiring in one place. This should reduce drift between Telegram buttons, text commands, web actions and a future MUD gateway.
- Service boundary and duplication cleanup: keep extracting behavior-preserving helpers and split large mixed-responsibility files into clearer modules. Current hotspots are `worldTick.ts`, `status.ts`, `actionCompletions.ts`, `statusServer.ts`, `locations.ts` and `aliases.ts`; see `docs/planning/items/TECH-001-service-boundary-and-duplication-cleanup.md`.
- Collision handling for gathering, eating, attacks, freshening and similar actions. If several players or creatures target the same resource, corpse or creature, completion should re-check and claim the target atomically so only one result wins. First version can resolve contested actions with a simple random roll; later versions can weigh speed, stamina, relevant skill, tool quality, creature size/strength, awareness and surprise. Losing actors should get a clear message such as the resource was already taken, the prey moved/died first, or the corpse is no longer usable; nearby characters should see an appropriate local message when the collision is visible.
- Refactor `src/services/actionQueue.ts`: split the large queue/orchestration file into smaller action-specific modules and clarify action completion boundaries.
- Map verticality / inside exits: add a small authored map update that introduces diegetic vertical or interior movement instead of keeping every meaningful place on `z=0`. Examples: a climbable tree with an `UP` exit to a high branch/lookout, a bear den or similar lair with an `INSIDE` exit into a real interior location, and a pit/ravine/hole with a `DOWN` exit to a lower place. Keep the first pass narrow and hand-authored, update `docs/world/world_map.md`, and make the entry features inspectable so players understand why they can climb, enter or descend.
- Map docs drift check: add `map:check` and CI/pre-commit coverage so changes under `prisma/data/world/` fail when `docs/world/world_map.md` was not regenerated.
- Character card expansion: name cases, name approval status, registration date/time, active play time, and current money.
- Character achievements / titles: add a `Відзнаки` or `Титули` view with categories, pagination, earned dates and short diegetic descriptions. Use existing stats first, then expand to skills, story events, exploration, personal chronicle discoveries, fishing, hunting, gathering and rare world events. Avoid generic MMO level/mob-copy language; prefer Chornolis-flavored milestones such as first return from danger, first useful find, first witnessed omen, first personal chronicle entry, first fish, careful hunter, bridge-walker, listener at the fire, etc. Some achievements can unlock visible titles, but titles should feel like names people might actually use in-world.
- Inventory item actions: the first dedicated inventory screen now has modest `Використати`, `Роздивитися`, `Викинути` and `Запалити факел` actions for resource stacks. Next, replace the temporary resource-stack drop model with real item instances so dropped objects preserve origin, quality, timers, freshness and richer pickup behavior. Dropped items should be visible immediately when light/visibility allows, and discoverable after `Роздивитися` when the location is dark. Reuse the loose-item pickup path where practical: ground items should be picked up directly into inventory, without gather chance rolls or action delays unless the character is exhausted or otherwise unable to act. Lost or dropped items, including torches, should not be silently re-added on ordinary bot startup, `/start` or `/reset`; they should come from world objects, authored seed/reset state or explicit admin/scribe tools.
- First use for gathered resources: the 0.11.12 first pass lets berries restore a small amount of stamina, mushrooms ease hunger, and herbs restore a small amount of HP from the inventory screen. Later, add richer item details, better wound/status hooks, other foods, stronger food/survival tradeoffs, and stronger links into cooking, medicine, herbalism, quests and crafting. Keep effects modest and diegetic so gathering matters without replacing rest, medicine or future cooking.
- Future potions and prepared remedies: later crafting/alchemy/herbalism should turn gathered resources into stronger effects such as fuller Життя/Снага recovery, sharper perception, seeing in darkness, masking scent or presence from animals, and other temporary perception/stealth states. These should require recipes, tools, skill, location context or NPC teaching rather than raw item clicks.
- Equipment and visible appearance: inspecting another character should eventually reflect worn clothing, carried packs, visible weapons, tools and other obvious gear instead of only the current torch/no-weapon placeholder. Better observation, tracking, medicine, combat or social skills can unlock richer descriptions such as posture, fatigue, wounds, weapon confidence, quality of clothes, hidden pockets or suspicious burdens.
- NPC inventory and carried tools: add a real inventory/equipment model for creatures and NPCs, then seed знахарям one carried torch each instead of placing loose torches near their starting locations. NPCs should be able to visibly carry simple tools and supplies, and inspection should show obvious carried items when appropriate.
- Torch lifecycle beyond the current starter loop: lit torches should eventually consume the underlying torch item, be extinguishable to save remaining burn time, be craftable from suitable materials, and support limited-stock torch stands or bundles instead of the current infinite gate source. NPCs such as знахарі should eventually carry, use, craft, pick up or restock torches as part of their route when they see available torches and are able to take them.
- Individual perishable item model: replace temporary resource-stack corpses with real item instances that can carry freshness, decay, origin creature and container/location state.
- Real active time tracking for players based on actions/activity rather than account age, after the day/night loop and world-time service exist. Do not show a placeholder line in the player profile until this can report meaningful in-world time.
- Dynamic `/time` model after the static starter date: season, moon circle/month, day and time of day should advance with world time.
- Presence reveal near campfires and later in daylight: the first campfire/torch reveal pass exists, but daylight/darkness rules should later use the same visibility layer so names, animal labels and interaction buttons appear only when conditions allow it.
- Weather and weather effects on visibility, travel, gathering, tracks, rest, fire and creature behavior.
- Contacts, groups, kurені/січі or similar social organization tools.
- Richer NPC and creature reactions to signals: the first basic pass lets living animals sometimes flee from a directed social signal. Later reactions should account for species, hunger, fear, recent attacks, relationship/reputation, posture and signal tone. Examples: a person or NPC may look back, nod, smile and step away, ignore the gesture, or answer with a signal; a fox might startle and flee after a wave; a wolf or other predator might snap its jaws at the air, threaten, stalk or attack after an aggressive signal such as a glare. These reactions should write visible local events and avoid turning every signal into guaranteed movement.
- Combat system beyond the current simple animal attack/debug kill flow.
- Unarmed combat skill and attack methods: the current small-prey result can say that the character `затоптали` the animal, but queued/visible action text should remain the broader `атакує/атакуємо`. Later, add attack method selection and relevant skills so unarmed trampling, fists, knives, spears, bows, traps and improvised tools can produce different messages, costs, risks and learning.
- Opponent assessment: approximate comparison between the player and a character, animal, monster or spirit before conflict, after attributes, states and skills exist. When examining a target such as `Вправний мисливець Іван охороняє східні ворота табору`, offer an assessment option and command that estimates whether attacking would be trivial, risky, evenly matched, dangerous or practically impossible.
- Newcomer helper / tutorial guide: promoted to `ONB-001` in `next` because people are already interested. Keep the backlog reminder here only for future expansion beyond the first playable guide.
- Keep onboarding current: whenever a new early-game feature ships, review `/start`, `/help`, keyboards, fallback hints and the newcomer-helper/tutorial plan so beginner guidance teaches the current game rather than an older version of it.
- Dream tutorial after onboarding: the character sleeps and may learn basic controls in a dream, with an option to skip and wake up.
- Cowardice/flee settings: player-configurable health threshold for trying to flee combat.
- Water sources as location features: wells, springs, streams and other visible places where characters can later drink, fill containers, fish, wash, set ambushes or notice animal tracks.
- Food and hunger system beyond the current simple debug hunger counter: make hunger actively matter; add edible berries as an early gathered food that can be eaten from inventory, reduces hunger, and later slightly eases thirst once thirst exists. Add edible bread, basic cooking around ovens/печі, and cooked meat from freshened corpses. Fresh meat should come from `Освіжувати` a fresh corpse, then be roastable at a crafted campfire/костер or suitable fire feature, with cooking/firemaking/survival skills affecting quality, time and failure.
- Player hunger over world time: current player hunger is a simple action-pressure counter capped to 13 and grows when stamina-spending actions are completed. Later it should advance with passing in-game hours, rest/sleep, eating and exertion, with clear but not overly punishing thresholds.
- Thirst and water: when water sources and thirst exist, show thirst like hunger (`Вас мучить спрага` with weak/medium/strong intensity), make water sources matter, and avoid exposing exact thirst numbers outside local technical details.
- Observing carried wealth and things: by default, inspecting another player should not reveal their money, inventory or hidden carried items. Later add diegetic ways to notice visible objects, purses, packs, dropped items, theft/trade clues or scribe/debug detail views.
- Visible reputation and achievements: instead of always showing raw player statistics from the outside, later show earned signs such as traveled far, skilled gatherer, careful hunter or other irregularly refreshed achievements/titles. Avoid expensive always-on ranking queries; compute or refresh these summaries opportunistically.

## Promotion rules

Move an item from backlog to `next` when:

- the needed foundation exists;
- the task can be broken into small implementation steps;
- it supports the current roadmap phase;
- it does not distract from the atmospheric MVP.
