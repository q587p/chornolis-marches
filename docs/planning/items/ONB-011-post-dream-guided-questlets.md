# ONB-011 — Post-Tutorial Dream Guided Questlets

## Goal

After the tutorial dream, add a short series of diegetic beginner questlets that continue teaching the waking world without turning Chornolis into a checklist RPG.

## Timing

Not now. This should wait until the first-session loop, beginner safety, light/darkness, tracking, freshening/cooking learning and starter-area affordances feel stable.

## Direction

- Use small local tasks that teach attention and survival through play:
  - take light before darkness;
  - look/examine signs and features;
  - follow a fresh trace;
  - gather or cook once;
  - return to camp safely;
  - notice a local person, animal or spirit sign.
- Keep tasks optional and atmospheric. The world may nudge, remember or answer, but it should not spam fixed quest markers.
- Prefer local characters, signs, dreams, boards or environmental prompts over an abstract quest log.
- Let the player complete steps by doing ordinary actions, not by accepting a formal quest state first.

## Guardrails

- Do not add this inside the tutorial dream itself.
- Do not add broad quest journal UI in the first slice.
- Do not make the settlement gate notice board a generic quest board.
- Do not gate core survival behind quest completion.
- Do not reveal hidden routes, exact map solutions or raw mechanics.
- Do not make rewards large enough to become a farming loop.

## First Slice Ideas

- A single post-dream nudge from Сон/Дрімота or a starter-camp sign pointing toward light, looking around and returning safely.
- A local "first trail" prompt that uses existing track/follow attention without requiring auto-follow.
- A small camp-return memory after the player finds one nearby landmark and comes back.

## Acceptance Notes

- The first questlet series teaches through ordinary commands and world interactions.
- It remains optional, skippable and low-pressure.
- It does not become a standard quest log, bounty board or checklist.
- Beginner guidance stays diegetic and does not expose raw systems.
