# TRAVEL-001 — Horses And Mounted Travel

## Goal

After the world has distant zones where walking takes meaningfully long, add horses or similar mounted travel so long-distance movement can become a real survival/logistics choice instead of only repeated step commands.

## Timing

Keep this cold until:

- the playable world has far zones that are intentionally slow to reach on foot;
- route-finding and travel-group foundations are stable enough to reason about multi-step travel;
- animal handling, rest, light, danger and fatigue rules can support mounts without turning them into free fast travel.

## Direction

- Horses should shorten long routes, not replace the meaning of local exploration.
- Mounted travel should still care about roads, terrain, darkness, weather, danger, fatigue, wounds and supplies.
- A horse should be a living presence: it can tire, refuse unsafe routes, need care, be led, tied, lost, stolen or injured once those systems exist.
- Distant travel should be explicit and interruptible. Do not add silent teleportation or guaranteed route replay.
- Mounted travel should integrate with future travel groups rather than creating a parallel party system.

## First Slice Ideas

- Lead or mount a horse in a safe authored location.
- Move faster only on known/open road-like routes.
- Refuse or warn on dense forest, steep/vertical exits, hidden passages, dreams, narrow crawls and dangerous terrain.
- Show qualitative condition: calm, tired, nervous, wounded, hungry or unwilling.

## Non-Goals

- No horses before distant zones make them useful.
- No fast travel.
- No mounted combat.
- No breeding, trade, stables or economy loop in the first slice.
- No automatic travel across hidden routes or unknown paths.

## Acceptance Notes

- Mounted travel remains a future long-distance system, not a beginner shortcut.
- The first implementation should prove a small safe route before adding ownership, stables, mounted combat or economy.
