# 0.10.9 — Animal pressure and ecology danger

## Summary

This patch makes dense animal populations and attacks affect the local ecology more visibly, while reducing queue pressure from large migrations.

## Fixed

- Creature action batches are larger than player batches, so animal-heavy worlds can catch up faster without eating the player lane.
- Ordinary animal movement no longer sends departure/arrival messages to Telegram; tracks are still created, and NPC/special creature movement remains visible.

## Ecology

- Crowded locations now gain dynamic ecology danger when more than 13 creatures or players are present.
- Crowd danger suppresses herbivore reproduction and makes herbivores more likely to leave over several ticks.
- Attacks now add temporary local ecology danger, so nearby herbivores are more likely to scatter for several world ticks after violence.

## Planning

- Added a backlog follow-up for visible animal migration through signs, tracks, `/stat` deltas or opt-in/debug views instead of raw Telegram spam.
- Added Icebox notes for future species-specific fear, aggression and offspring-defense behavior.
