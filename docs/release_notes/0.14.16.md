# 0.14.16 - Ecology recovery follow-ups

Date: 12026-06-01

## Summary

This release follows the 0.14.15 starter ecology rebalance with two conservative recovery valves. Starter mice and rabbits can now be restored when the world still has living members of the species but no adult breeding pair remains. Hungry predators can also scavenge safe unclaimed herbivore corpses before attacking live prey.

## Player-Facing Notes

- Small prey populations should have a better chance to recover instead of quietly dead-ending after one sex or all adult pairs disappear.
- Hungry predators remain dangerous, but they can now use suitable abandoned carcasses as food pressure before immediately hunting live prey.
- Hunter-claimed, player-carried and already freshened corpses are still protected from this scavenging path.

## Technical Notes

- `planPopulationFloorRestoration()` now accepts adult breeding-sex counts and applies no-pair restoration only to rabbits and mice.
- `restorePopulationFloors()` now reads living animal age/sex data to build those breeding counts during world ticks.
- Predator feeding helpers now centralize prey food value and ordinary scavengable-corpse eligibility.
- Carnivore ticks queue `EAT` for unclaimed local herbivore corpses before selecting a live prey target when hunger pressure is active.

## Validation

- `node scripts/test/population-restoration.cjs`
- `node scripts/test/predator-feeding.cjs`

## Risks

- The no-pair restoration remains a mechanical safeguard, not a full migration/refuge system.
- Predator scavenging uses local visible corpse records only; richer scavenger behavior, scent tracking and contested carcasses remain future ecology work.
