# 0.15.0 - Nocturnal owl ecology

Date: 12026-06-01

## Summary

This early `0.15.x` ecology patch adds owls as a small nocturnal predator to help relieve mouse pressure while keeping the scope deliberately narrow. Owls reuse the existing creature species, starter animal, predator and world tick systems without schema or migration changes.

## Player-Facing Notes

- Owls now exist in the waking world as a small new nighttime presence.
- During the day, owls hide and sleep instead of acting in the ordinary creature queue.
- At dawn, dusk and night, owls wake, unhide and can hunt.
- Owls strongly prefer mice. They may rarely threaten child or young rabbits, but they do not add a broad new predator loop in this patch.

## Technical Notes

- Added the `owl` creature species as `ANIMAL` / `CARNIVORE`.
- Added `owl` to starter animal species keys and seeded three starter owls through `STARTER_PREDATORS`.
- Added `isOwlActiveDaypart(daypart)` and a world tick DB sync that sleeps/hides owls during day and wakes/unhides them during active dayparts.
- Daytime owl sync cancels queued or running owl actions so sleeping owls do not keep acting through daylight.
- Added owl predator preference weights: mouse `100`, child rabbit `18`, young rabbit `8`, otherwise `0`.
- Added owl attack base chance `42`.
- Added an extra creature attack completion guard so hidden or sleeping owls cannot finish an already loaded attack after daytime sync cancels their queued/running actions.
- Kept breeding-pair restoration limited to rabbits and mice. Owls can still use ordinary starter population-floor restoration if the starter owl population disappears entirely.
- Did not add flight, nests, eggs, ranged hunting, owl reproduction, Prisma schema changes or migrations.

## Risks

- Owl tuning is intentionally first-pass: three starter owls may still be too few or too many depending on live mouse pressure and predator starvation.
- Owls hide by day through a tick-level sync, so unusual admin/debug time jumps should be watched for stale hidden/sleeping state.
- Owl movement is still ordinary creature movement. This patch does not model flight, perches, nests or special vertical pathing.
- Owl reproduction is not implemented; starter population-floor restoration is a safeguard, not a natural breeding system.
- Predator targeting is still a local tick behavior and does not yet include scent, sound, distant hunting routes or contested prey.
